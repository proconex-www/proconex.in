Supply & Repair of OBSOLETE Panels Which are not supported by the OEMS

Providing maintenance and repair services for outdated panels lacking support from original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) is a specialized yet frequently essential industry. In this domain, entities focusing on the provision and restoration of obsolete panels are commonly known as “third-party maintenance” or “independent service providers”.

Identification of Obsolete Panels

These companies specialize in identifying and sourcing obsolete panels that may no longer be available through the original equipment manufacturers. They often have a network of suppliers or sources for acquiring discontinued or hard-to-find components.

Inventory Management

Maintaining an inventory of obsolete panels is crucial. This involves acquiring, storing, and managing a stock of discontinued components to meet the needs of clients in need of replacements or repairs.

Repair Services

These companies often have skilled technicians who can repair malfunctioning or damaged panels. They might reverse engineer components, troubleshoot issues, and provide cost-effective repair solutions.

Custom Manufacturing

In some cases, when replacement parts are not available, these companies might offer custom manufacturing services. This involves creating replicas or compatible components to replace obsolete ones.

Technical Support

Providing technical support for clients dealing with obsolete equipment. This can involve offering advice on maintenance, troubleshooting, and integrating replacement components.

Cost-Effective Solutions

One of the key advantages of third-party maintenance is often cost savings. Clients can avoid the high costs associated with upgrading entire systems or replacing equipment by opting for repairs or compatible replacements.

Documentation and Certification

Ensuring that the supplied or repaired components meet industry standards and providing appropriate documentation. This is important for clients who may need to comply with regulatory requirements.

Collaboration with Original Equipment Manufacturers (OEMs)

Some third-party maintenance providers collaborate with OEMs to gain access to technical specifications, documentation, or even official support in cases where OEMs are willing to work with independent service providers.
