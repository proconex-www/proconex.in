Our Services

  • Diagnostic Analysis: Our team of skilled technicians utilizes state-of-the-art diagnostic equipment to accurately assess the condition of your automation and control parts. We identify the root cause of the issue to provide targeted repair solutions.
  • Component Repair: Whether it’s a malfunctioning PLC, HMI, servo drive, or any other automation component, we have the expertise to repair it promptly and effectively. Our technicians are trained to work with various brands and models, ensuring that your equipment is restored to optimal functionality.
  • Board-Level Repair: We specialize in board-level repair, addressing issues such as faulty circuitry, damaged components, and connectivity problems. By repairing individual boards rather than replacing entire units, we help our clients minimize costs and reduce downtime.
  • Component Testing: Before returning repaired parts to our clients, we conduct rigorous testing to ensure that they meet our quality standards and perform reliably in the field. Our testing procedures include functional testing, performance evaluation, and quality assurance checks.
  • Preventive Maintenance: In addition to repair services, we offer preventive maintenance programs to help our clients maximize the lifespan and performance of their automation and control parts. Through regular inspections and maintenance tasks, we identify potential issues early and implement proactive solutions to prevent costly breakdowns.
  • On-Site Support: For urgent repairs or situations where shipping components is not feasible, we offer on-site repair services. Our technicians can travel to your facility equipped with the necessary tools and expertise to diagnose and repair issues on-site, minimizing downtime and disruptions to your operations.

Supply & Repair of OBSOLETE Panels Which are not supported by the OEMS

Providing maintenance and repair services for outdated panels lacking support from original equipment manufacturers (OEMs) is a specialized yet frequently essential industry. In this domain, entities focusing on the provision and restoration of obsolete panels are commonly known as “third-party maintenance” or “independent service providers”.

Identification of Obsolete Panels

Our company specializes in identifying and sourcing obsolete panels that may no longer be available through the original equipment manufacturers. We have a network of suppliers and sources dedicated to acquiring discontinued or hard-to-find components.

Inventory Management

We are maintaining a crucial inventory of obsolete panels, which involves acquiring, storing, and managing a stock of discontinued components. This ensures we can meet the needs of clients requiring replacements or repairs.

Repair Services

Our company comprises skilled technicians and process control experts who excel in repairing malfunctioning or damaged panels. With expertise in reverse engineering components, troubleshooting issues, and delivering cost-effective repair solutions, we ensure comprehensive support for your needs.

Custom Manufacturing

In certain instances, when replacement parts are unavailable, our company may offer custom manufacturing services. This entails creating replicas or compatible components to replace obsolete ones, providing customized options and solutions for your specific needs.

Technical Support

Our company offers technical support for clients dealing with obsolete equipment. This includes providing advice on maintenance, troubleshooting, and integrating replacement components to ensure seamless operations.

Cost-Effective Solutions

Opting for third-party maintenance offers a key advantage, often resulting in cost savings. Clients can bypass the high costs associated with upgrading entire systems or replacing equipment by choosing repairs or compatible replacements.

Documentation and Certification

Our commitment involves ensuring that the supplied or repaired components not only meet but surpass industry standards, accompanied by appropriate documentation. This dedication is particularly significant for clients who need to comply with regulatory requirements.

Collaboration with Original Equipment Manufacturers (OEMs)

Collaborating with OEMs, some third-party maintenance providers seek access to technical specifications, documentation, or even official support. This occurs in cases where OEMs are willing to work with independent service providers, fostering a collaborative environment for enhanced technical support.
