Load Commutated Inverter

A Load Commutated Inverter (LCI) is a type of inverter used in high-power applications, particularly in high-voltage direct current (HVDC) transmission systems. The LCI is a type of thyristor-based inverter that converts direct current (DC) into alternating current (AC). It is commonly employed in HVDC systems for transmitting electrical power over long distances with minimal losses.

Here are the key features and components of a Load Commutated Inverter:

  • Thyristors: The LCI relies on thyristors (also known as silicon-controlled rectifiers or SCR) as the switching devices. Thyristors are semiconductor devices that can control the flow of electric current.
  • Commutation: Unlike other types of inverters, the LCI uses natural commutation to turn off the thyristors. Commutation refers to the process of turning off a conducting thyristor. In an LCI, the commutation process relies on the natural reversal of current in the AC circuit, allowing the thyristor to turn off.
  • Control System: The LCI requires a sophisticated control system to ensure proper commutation and control of the AC output. The control system monitors the current and voltage waveforms, adjusting the firing angles of the thyristors to maintain a stable and controlled output.
  • Filtering Components: In HVDC applications, it is crucial to filter out harmonics and other unwanted components from the AC output. Filtering components such as reactors and capacitors are often incorporated into the LCI system to improve the quality of the AC waveform.
  • Cooling System: Due to the high power levels involved, LCIs often require efficient cooling systems to dissipate heat generated by the thyristors during operation. This may involve the use of air or liquid cooling methods.
  • Transformer: An LCI system may include transformers to step up or step down the voltage as required for the specific HVDC transmission system.

LCIs are commonly used in HVDC applications where long-distance power transmission is necessary. They offer advantages in terms of controllability, reliability, and efficiency in high-power scenarios. However, they also require careful design and control to ensure proper commutation and stable operation.

The LCI operates using reliable load-commutated, phase-controlled power thyristor technology to power the stator windings of a highly efficient synchronous motor. It features a power circuit with a source converter connected to the power supply and a load converter connected to the motor. Power flows from the source to the motor during motoring, and built-in regeneration allows smooth load control. The LCI regulates motor speed by controlling motor torque through the DC link current. The power converter blocks can be paralleled for ratings exceeding 70,000 HP, offering low harmonics and the option for single-channel operation. For custom systems, consult TMEIC General Industries to meet specific requirements.

Spares

To maintain an industrial Load Commutated Inverter (LCI) system, we have a supply of spare parts and equipment to ensure the reliability and continuous operation of the system. Here is a list of common LCI industrial spares and equipment:

  • Thyristors (SCRs): As mentioned earlier, thyristors are key components in the LCI system. Keep a stock of spare thyristors to replace any that may fail during operation.
  • Gate Firing Circuit Boards: These boards control the firing of the thyristors. Having spare gate firing circuit boards is important for quick replacement in case of malfunctions.
  • Control System Components: Maintain spare parts for the control system, including microprocessors, control cards, communication modules, and any other components that contribute to the monitoring and control of the LCI system.
  • Current and Voltage Sensors: Sensors monitoring the current and voltage are crucial for the control system to make informed decisions. Keep spare sensors to ensure accurate monitoring.
  • Cooling System Components: Depending on the LCI system's design, keep spare parts for cooling systems, such as pumps, fans, heat exchangers, and temperature sensors.
  • Filtering Components: Spare reactors, capacitors, and other filtering components are essential for maintaining the quality of the AC waveform and complying with power quality standards.
  • Protection Devices: Maintain a supply of spare fuses, circuit breakers, and other protection devices to safeguard the LCI system from overloads or faults.
  • Transformer Components: If the LCI system includes transformers, have spare transformer components like bushings, tap changers, and insulation materials.
  • Communication Equipment: Spare communication modules, transceivers, and related equipment are essential for ensuring proper communication within the LCI system.
  • Monitoring and Diagnostic Tools: Keep spare diagnostic tools, such as oscilloscopes and power analyzers, to facilitate troubleshooting and maintenance activities.
  • Documentation: Include spare documentation such as manuals, schematics, and operation guides for quick reference during maintenance and repairs.

Test & Certification

For Load Commutated Inverter (LCI) industrial spares and equipment, conducting thorough testing and certification processes is essential to ensure their reliability and compliance with industry standards. The testing and certification procedures typically involve the following steps:

  • Functional Testing: Perform functional tests on each spare part and equipment to ensure that they operate according to their specified functions. This includes testing the thyristors, gate firing circuit boards, control system components, sensors, and other critical elements.
  • Performance Testing: Assess the performance of the LCI system and its components under various operating conditions. Verify that the equipment meets or exceeds the specified performance parameters, such as power output, efficiency, and response time.
  • Safety Testing: Prioritize safety by conducting tests to verify that the equipment complies with relevant safety standards. This includes testing protection devices, insulation, and other safety features to ensure they function correctly.
  • Environmental Testing: Subject the equipment to environmental tests to evaluate its resilience under different conditions, including temperature variations, humidity, and vibration. This helps ensure that the equipment can withstand the operating environment in industrial settings.
  • Endurance Testing: Test the equipment for extended periods under normal operating conditions to assess its long-term reliability and durability. This is crucial for identifying potential issues that may arise over time.
  • Certification Process: Work with recognized certification bodies or agencies to obtain certifications that validate the compliance of the LCI spares and equipment with industry standards. Certifications from reputable organizations provide assurance of quality and adherence to safety regulations.
  • Documentation: Maintain comprehensive documentation for the testing and certification processes, including test reports, certificates, and compliance documentation. This documentation is crucial for audits, quality assurance, and regulatory compliance.
  • Periodic Recertification: Regularly review and update certifications to ensure continued compliance with evolving industry standards and regulations. Periodic recertification demonstrates a commitment to maintaining high-quality and safe equipment.

When considering the testing and certification of LCI industrial spares and equipment, it is advisable to consult with industry experts, adhere to relevant standards (such as IEEE and IEC standards for power electronics), and collaborate with certification bodies to ensure thorough and credible assessments.
